# Engineering - Axes

This document defines the axes of progression for all roles within the Engineering discipline, including **Software Engineers**, **SRE/DevOps Engineers**, and **QA Engineers**. It provides role-specific descriptions for each axis and level across both IC and Manager tracks.

### 1. System

#### Software Engineer

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| :---- | :---------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Focuses on understanding a specific part of the codebase, working on well-defined tasks with detailed specifications. Their mental model is localized to a single module or feature, and they require guidance to see how their changes connect to the broader system. Success means implementing tasks safely within their immediate scope.      |
| 2     | Contributor | Grasps how multiple components and services interact to deliver a feature. Can extend or modify small systems with minimal supervision, understanding dependencies, API contracts, and basic failure modes. They can trace a request's flow through several familiar services to debug issues effectively.                                       |
| 3     | Owner       | Independently owns a subsystem or service, designing features that scale and operate reliably within its boundaries. They make thoughtful trade-offs between complexity, performance, and maintainability, author clear design documents, and proactively identify technical risks and debt before they become major problems.                    |
| 4     | Architect   | Designs robust and scalable solutions that span multiple systems and teams. They anticipate future scaling, reliability, and security needs, aligning technical decisions with organizational architecture patterns. They influence other teamsâ€™ roadmaps to ensure a cohesive, long-term technical strategy.                             |
| 5     | Strategist  | Shapes the system architecture across entire domains, defining a long-term vision that connects technical strategy to business outcomes. They balance competing priorities, pioneer new architectural standards, and influence multiple teams to align on system-wide principles that enable future innovation.                              |

#### SRE/DevOps

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| :---- | :---------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Learns a specific piece of infrastructure, like a CI job or an alerting rule, following established procedures for routine tasks. Their understanding is component-level, and they require guidance to grasp how their actions impact the broader production environment. Their focus is on executing tasks safely and correctly.                                                             |
| 2     | Contributor | Manages and troubleshoots a single infrastructure component, like a monitoring agent or a single host. They can modify existing automation scripts and understand the direct dependencies of the component they manage, but have a limited view of the entire system's topology. They can effectively resolve incidents within their area of ownership.                                  |
| 3     | Owner       | Owns a complete infrastructure service, like the logging pipeline or a caching tier. They design and implement automation to improve its reliability, performance, and scalability, making informed trade-offs between cost and availability and proactively identifying operational risks within that service.                                                                            |
| 4     | Architect   | Designs and builds new, complex infrastructure systems, such as a container orchestration platform or a continuous deployment system from scratch. Their work improves the reliability, performance, and efficiency of many services, not just a single one, and they provide leverage for the entire engineering organization.                                                            |
| 5     | Strategist  | Defines the long-term strategy for the organization's entire infrastructure and reliability. They drive initiatives that fundamentally improve how the company builds and operates software, influencing architectural decisions across all of engineering to ensure resilience, observability, and scalability are core tenets.                                                          |

#### QA

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :---- | :---------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Understands the test scope for a single, isolated feature. They execute pre-written test cases accurately, clearly document the results, and can follow a bug's lifecycle. Their system view is limited to the user-facing functionality they are assigned to test, and they are learning to think about edge cases.                                                                               |
| 2     | Contributor | Designs and writes test cases for small-to-medium features that may interact with other components. They can identify and isolate bugs in familiar areas of the application and understand the data flow between a few connected services. They begin to think about how different parts of the system can fail.                                                                                   |
| 3     | Owner       | Owns the complete test strategy for a service or a significant feature area. They design and implement robust automated tests and frameworks, analyzing the system's architecture to identify potential quality risks and focusing testing efforts where they will have the most impact. They move beyond finding bugs to preventing them.                                                          |
| 4     | Architect   | Designs and implements organization-wide quality standards, tools, and frameworks. They introduce new testing methodologies (e.g., contract testing, chaos engineering) that improve product quality across many teams, influencing application architecture to improve testability and reduce risk.                                                                                               |
| 5     | Strategist  | Defines the company's overall quality and testing philosophy. They drive a holistic culture of quality across engineering, product, and design, ensuring that quality is not just about testing but is a core part of the entire development lifecycle, from requirements and design to monitoring in production.                                                                                |

### 2. Mastery

#### Software Engineer

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| :---- | :----------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the syntax and basic libraries of at least one programming language, along with fundamental debugging, testing, and version control. Focuses on applying established standards and team conventions with guidance, developing good habits in the process.                                                                                |
| 2     | Practitioner | Independently builds features using common frameworks and libraries. They are proficient in the team's primary tools and languages, writing clean, idiomatic code and consistently applying conventions. They can debug moderately complex issues in familiar areas and understand basic performance and security considerations.                |
| 3     | Specialist   | Is recognized as an expert in one or more technical domains, such as backend APIs or frontend frameworks. They consistently balance performance, maintainability, and security in their solutions, and their peers seek their input on complex technical decisions, library choices, and debugging challenging issues.                          |
| 4     | Expert       | Demonstrates broad, multi-faceted experience across various technologies and domains. They improve team productivity by creating or enhancing shared libraries, tools, and frameworks. They can rapidly evaluate new technologies with critical judgment and effectively troubleshoot the most complex, unfamiliar technical problems. |
| 5     | Innovator    | Advances the state of the art for engineering practices inside and outside the company. They introduce new paradigms, contribute to significant open-source projects, or shape industry standards. Their work creates foundational shifts that unlock new capabilities for the entire organization.                                       |

#### SRE/DevOps

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :----------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the fundamentals of a core technology (e.g., Kubernetes, AWS) and a common scripting language (e.g., Bash, Python). Their focus is on mastering basic commands and concepts to execute routine operational tasks safely and consistently.                                                                                                   |
| 2     | Practitioner | Is proficient in the team's core infrastructure tools. They can write and debug automation scripts and infrastructure-as-code (e.g., Terraform) for moderately complex tasks, such as provisioning a new service with supervision. They are effective at using observability tools to diagnose common production issues.                              |
| 3     | Specialist   | Is an expert in a specific domain of infrastructure, such as observability, container orchestration, or networking. They apply this deep knowledge to optimize systems for performance, cost, and reliability, and are a go-to person for complex incident response in their area of expertise.                                                       |
| 4     | Expert       | Has deep expertise across multiple infrastructure domains. They design and build robust, scalable, and easy-to-use tools and platforms that improve productivity and safety for the entire engineering organization, abstracting away complex infrastructure concerns.                                                                               |
| 5     | Innovator    | Pioneers new approaches to infrastructure and reliability that are adopted by the broader industry. They are recognized externally as an expert, and their work helps define the technical vision for how the company will scale its operations for years to come.                                                                                    |

#### QA

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :----------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the fundamentals of different testing types (e.g., manual, automated, performance) and the specific tools used by the team. Their focus is on developing a strong foundation in quality principles and executing assigned testing tasks with a high degree of accuracy and attention to detail.                                                  |
| 2     | Practitioner | Is proficient in writing automated tests using the team's frameworks. They can debug test failures, identify the underlying cause of a defect, and contribute to the existing test automation codebase with confidence. They understand how to balance testing thoroughness with speed of feedback.                                                    |
| 3     | Specialist   | Is an expert in a specific area of quality, such as performance testing, security testing, or a complex part of the product. They design and implement sophisticated test strategies to address challenging quality problems, and they coach other engineers on how to improve their testing skills in this area.                                        |
| 4     | Expert       | Has deep expertise across multiple quality domains. They introduce new tools and practices that significantly improve the organization's testing capabilities, such as implementing a new type of testing framework or a new process for analyzing production quality data. They are a force multiplier for quality.                               |
| 5     | Innovator    | Is an industry leader in software quality. They develop new testing techniques and paradigms that advance the state of the art, often sharing their work through blog posts, conference talks, or open source projects. They help shape the long-term vision for how the company delivers high-quality software at scale.                           |

### 3. Quality

#### Software Engineer

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :---- | :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Follows           | Produces functional code that meets the primary requirements, relying on code reviews and close feedback to improve clarity, testing, and maintainability. Their focus is on correctness for the "happy path," and they are learning to anticipate edge cases and write basic, effective tests.                                                       |
| 2     | Consistent        | Consistently produces maintainable, well-tested, and clearly documented code that requires little rework. They anticipate straightforward edge cases, handle them gracefully, and view testing as an integral part of their development process, selecting the appropriate testing methods for their changes.                                          |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Produces robust, well-tested, and clear systems that serve as examples for others. They improve team practices by providing constructive, detailed feedback in reviews, anticipating subtle edge cases, and championing efforts to improve code clarity, test coverage, and comprehensive documentation.                                            |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Introduces frameworks, automation, or practices that multiply quality across the team or department. They might build a CI/CD pipeline that prevents common errors or a library that makes secure coding the default. They proactively tackle technical debt and coach peers on testing, resilience, and clear design.                            |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Establishes and shapes company-wide quality standards that raise the organizational bar. They champion guidelines for readability, testing, performance, and documentation, often developing systemic strategies and tools that prevent entire classes of defects and outages.                                                                   |

#### SRE/DevOps

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| :---- | :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Follows           | Follows documented procedures carefully to avoid errors, relying on review and automation to prevent mistakes in production changes. Their focus is on precision and consistency, ensuring that every operational task is performed according to best practice.                                                                                          |
| 2     | Consistent        | Makes changes to production systems safely and reliably, with a clear understanding of the potential impact. They write clear documentation for operational procedures and contribute to improving existing runbooks. Their work is trusted to not cause incidents.                                                                                        |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Proactively identifies and mitigates risks to reliability before they cause an incident. They implement robust monitoring and alerting to detect issues before they impact users and use data from past incidents to drive meaningful improvements to system resilience.                                                                                    |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Designs and implements systems that improve the reliability of the entire platform, not just one service. This could include chaos engineering frameworks, automated incident response tooling, or SLO/SLI tracking systems. They make it easier for all engineers to build and operate reliable services.                                                 |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Drives a company-wide culture of reliability where every engineer feels ownership over production. They develop strategies and systems that make building reliable services the default path, not the exception. They are a key voice in ensuring the organization is meeting its commitments to customers.                                                  |

#### QA

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| :---- | :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Follows           | Executes test cases accurately and reports defects with clear, reproducible steps, ensuring that issues are well-understood by developers. They rely on feedback to improve their test case design and bug reporting skills. Their work provides a crucial signal about the state of the product.                                                           |
| 2     | Consistent        | Writes clear, concise, and comprehensive test cases that effectively find bugs. They consistently identify important defects and work collaboratively with developers to ensure they are resolved. They are a reliable source of information about the quality of the features they are responsible for.                                                         |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Champions a culture of quality on their team, moving beyond finding bugs to helping prevent them in the first place. They are involved early in the design process to identify quality risks and use data to help their team make informed decisions about what to test and when to release.                                                                   |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Designs and implements frameworks and infrastructure that enable all engineers, not just QA, to test their code more effectively. They might introduce a new end-to-end testing framework or build a tool for easily creating test data, multiplying their impact across the organization.                                                                   |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Establishes the company-wide bar for product quality. They drive a culture where every engineer feels ownership over the quality of their work and are a key partner to product and engineering leadership in making strategic decisions about risk and quality. They ensure the company's reputation for quality is a competitive advantage.       |

### 4. Execution

#### Software Engineer

| Level | Title         | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner       | Completes small, well-scoped tasks with close support and frequent communication. They are learning to break down problems into sequential steps, communicate progress effectively, and ask for help when blocked. Estimation and prioritization are developing skills.                                                                                |
| 2     | Delivers      | Delivers small to medium features independently and reliably. Manages their own workload effectively, breaks down tasks, provides reasonable estimates, and communicates risks early. They can handle a moderate degree of ambiguity and consistently follow through on their commitments.                                                                |
| 3     | Owner         | Reliably delivers medium to large projects from planning to production. They anticipate risks, mitigate blockers, and adapt plans as needed. Their ownership extends to ensuring the project is production-ready, well-documented, and sustainable, including stakeholder communication and managing dependencies.                                 |
| 4     | Orchestrates  | Coordinates and drives the delivery of complex projects across multiple contributors or teams. They align dependencies, manage priorities, and ensure consistent progress in the face of ambiguity. They are adept at decomposing large, unclear problems into actionable plans and keeping all stakeholders aligned.                          |
| 5     | Scales        | Defines and improves the organizational practices, processes, and tools for execution. They build systems that improve delivery speed and predictability across multiple teams, identify and remove systemic bottlenecks, and mentor others on how to lead large-scale projects effectively.                                                        |

#### SRE/DevOps

| Level | Title         | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :---- | :------------ | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner       | Completes routine operational tasks, such as handling alerts or running scripts, with guidance. They follow checklists and documented procedures to ensure correctness and are learning to manage their time effectively in a reactive environment.                                                                                                |
| 2     | Delivers      | Handles on-call incidents for a service with confidence and can independently complete moderately complex operational tasks. They can prioritize effectively during an incident and communicate clearly with stakeholders. They are a reliable member of the on-call rotation.                                                                   |
| 3     | Owner         | Leads the operational aspects of a major project, such as a service migration or a new product launch. They design and execute complex production changes with minimal risk, ensuring that the team is prepared for any potential issues. They own the operational readiness of the systems they support.                                              |
| 4     | Orchestrates  | Manages large, cross-team infrastructure projects, such as a major cloud provider upgrade or the rollout of a new observability platform. They are a calm and effective leader during major, multi-system outages, coordinating the incident response and ensuring that the team learns from the experience.                                        |
| 5     | Scales        | Implements processes and automation that improve the entire organization's ability to operate services reliably and efficiently. This might include building a new incident management framework, creating a new on-call training program, or developing a system for tracking and reducing operational toil.                                        |

#### QA

| Level | Title         | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner       | Completes assigned test execution tasks and reports status clearly and on time. They are learning to manage their testing workload and to communicate their progress effectively to the team. They are a reliable contributor to the team's testing efforts.                                                                                         |
| 2     | Delivers      | Can independently manage the testing for a small project, including creating a test plan, executing the tests, and reporting on the results. They are able to prioritize their work effectively to provide the most valuable quality signal to the team.                                                                                             |
| 3     | Owner         | Owns the quality for a large feature or product area. They define the test strategy, coordinate the execution of testing across multiple engineers (both QA and developers), and are accountable for the final quality of the release. They are a key partner to the product manager and tech lead.                                                      |
| 4     | Orchestrates  | Manages the quality assurance for a major product launch. They coordinate the efforts of many engineers across multiple teams to ensure a high-quality release, developing a comprehensive test strategy that covers everything from unit tests to user acceptance testing. They are a leader who can deliver quality at scale.                      |
| 5     | Scales        | Defines the processes and tools that the entire organization uses to ensure product quality. They improve the overall efficiency and effectiveness of the company's quality efforts, enabling teams to ship higher-quality software faster. They are a strategic leader who helps the company make smart investments in quality.                       |

### 5. People

| Level | Title           | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| :---- | :-------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner         | Communicates clearly with teammates and remains open to feedback, seeking help when stuck. They build trust by being reliable, listening actively in team discussions, and responding to feedback with a constructive, growth-oriented mindset.                                                                                                 |
| 2     | Contributor     | Actively participates in team rituals. Provides respectful, constructive feedback that helps teammates improve. Is a good pairing partner and informally shares knowledge, making the team more effective.                                                                                                                                   |
| 3     | Mentor          | Formally coaches teammates through pairing, thoughtful reviews, and knowledge-sharing sessions. Helps resolve team conflicts constructively, improves collaboration practices, and takes an active role in onboarding and supporting the growth of junior engineers.                                                                        |
| 4     | Leader          | Leads project teams by setting technical direction and managing contributors to achieve shared goals. They build consensus across groups, mediate disagreements, and actively support the growth of other mentors, fostering an inclusive environment and acting as a trusted voice in cross-functional forums.                                  |
| 5     | Culture Builder | Shapes a healthy, high-performing engineering culture across the organization. Champions collaboration, inclusion, and psychological safety. Acts as a role model for the company's values and plays a key role in attracting, retaining, and developing exceptional talent.                                                                    |

### 6. Influence

| Level | Title      | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| :---- | :--------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Local      | Work primarily impacts their immediate module or tasks. Their sphere of influence is their own work, and success is defined by learning how their individual contributions fit into the team's larger goals.                                                                                                                                     |
| 2     | Team       | Contributions improve the team's productivity and the reliability of its services. They own components or processes that teammates depend on, and their work directly enables the team to achieve its goals more effectively.                                                                                                                     |
| 3     | Multi-team | Work shapes shared infrastructure, tools, or practices that benefit multiple teams. They provide guidance outside their immediate scope and own systems that create leverage for other engineers, establishing best practices that are adopted more broadly.                                                                                       |
| 4     | Organization | Defines standards, frameworks, or platforms that have a significant, positive impact on the entire engineering organization. Their technical decisions and leadership on cross-cutting initiatives shape the direction of a large group of engineers and deliver substantial business value.                                                      |
| 5     | Industry   | Is recognized as an authority beyond the company for their technical contributions. They shape external communities and advance the state of the art through influential open-source work, publications, or conference presentations, enhancing both their own and the company's reputation.                                                       |
