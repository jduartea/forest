# Product - Axes

This document defines the axes of progression for all roles within the Product discipline, including **Product Managers** and **Product Designers**. It provides role-specific descriptions for each axis and level across both IC and Manager tracks.

### 1. Strategy

This axis describes the ability to define a clear and compelling vision for the product and to make decisions that drive long-term value for customers and the business.

#### Product Manager

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| :---- | :---------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Understands and can articulate the goals for a well-defined feature. They focus on understanding the "what" and "why" of a specific problem, relying on guidance from their manager to connect their work to broader product goals.                                                                                                            |
| 2     | Contributor | Understands and can articulate the strategy for their immediate team's product area. They connect their work to team-level objectives (e.g., OKRs) and can make trade-offs within a feature to better serve the team's goals, using data to inform their decisions.                                                                       |
| 3     | Owner       | Defines the strategy for a meaningful product area. They create a multi-quarter roadmap, set clear goals, and make complex prioritization trade-offs based on a deep understanding of user needs, business impact, and the competitive landscape. They are accountable for the success and outcomes of their product area.                 |
| 4     | Architect   | Defines the strategy for a large, ambiguous product area that spans multiple teams or products. They identify new, unproven opportunities, influence other teams' roadmaps to achieve their goals, and can successfully pitch and secure investment in major new initiatives. They are a recognized strategic leader.                          |
| 5     | Strategist  | Defines the product strategy for an entire business unit or a significant portion of the company's product portfolio. They identify and evaluate major new market opportunities and play a key role in shaping the company's long-term business and product strategy. Their work has a significant impact on the company's future success.    |

#### Product Designer

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| :---- | :---------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Understands the user goals and success metrics for a specific feature. They focus on executing design tasks that meet the immediate requirements, relying on the product manager and senior designers for strategic context.                                                                                                                |
| 2     | Contributor | Contributes to the strategic direction of their team's product area by bringing a deep understanding of user needs and behaviors. They can articulate how their design decisions support team-level objectives and can advocate for user-centered solutions.                                                                                     |
| 3     | Owner       | Owns the end-to-end user experience for a complex product area. They partner with the PM to define the problem and the vision, ensuring the user experience strategy aligns with the product strategy. They are accountable for the usability and desirability of their product area.                                                        |
| 4     | Architect   | Leads the design strategy for a large, ambiguous product area that spans multiple teams. They identify systemic UX problems and opportunities, and their work creates a cohesive and intuitive user experience across the entire customer journey. They are a strategic partner to product and engineering leaders.                        |
| 5     | Strategist  | Defines the company's overall design vision and strategy. They ensure that the company is delivering a world-class user experience that is a key differentiator in the market. Their work has a lasting impact on the company's brand, products, and culture.                                                                                       |

### 2. Mastery

This axis describes the depth of expertise in the craft and tools of product management or product design.

#### Product Manager

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :---- | :----------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the fundamentals of product management, including writing clear user stories, managing a backlog, and working effectively with an engineering team. They focus on developing core PM skills with guidance from their manager.                                                                                                                  |
| 2     | Practitioner | Is proficient in the core skills of product management. They can independently lead a product discovery process, conduct user interviews, analyze data to inform decisions, and manage a backlog to ensure the team is building the right thing.                                                                                                        |
| 3     | Specialist   | Is an expert in multiple product domains. They are highly skilled at complex prioritization, stakeholder management, and using a wide variety of quantitative and qualitative research methods to uncover user needs and business opportunities.                                                                                                            |
| 4     | Expert       | Improves product management practices across the organization. They may mentor other PMs, introduce new processes or tools, or establish best practices. They are often a deep expert in a strategic area for the company, such as pricing, growth, or a core technology platform.                                                               |
| 5     | Innovator    | Is recognized as an industry leader in product management. They may innovate on product practices, contribute to the broader product community, and help to shape the company's overall approach to building successful products. Their work helps to attract and retain top product talent.                                                          |

#### Product Designer

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :----------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the fundamentals of design, including core design tools (e.g., Figma) and the principles of UI and UX design. They can create mockups and prototypes from detailed specifications with guidance.                                                                                                                                                  |
| 2     | Practitioner | Is proficient in the end-to-end design process. They can independently conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and deliver high-quality visual designs for new features. They are a reliable contributor to the design of their team's product.                                                                                     |
| 3     | Specialist   | Owns the design for a complex product area. They create and maintain design systems to ensure consistency and quality. They are an expert in a specific design domain, such as interaction design, visual design, or user research, and are a go-to person for other designers in that area.                                                              |
| 4     | Expert       | Leads the design for large, multi-team projects. They solve systemic UX problems that span multiple product areas. They mentor other designers and play a key role in improving the organization's design practices, tools, and culture. They are a force multiplier for the design team.                                                                  |
| 5     | Innovator    | Defines the company's overall design vision and strategy. They are an industry leader in design, pushing the boundaries of the craft and helping to establish the company as a leader in design. Their work has a lasting impact on the company's products, brand, and culture.                                                                          |

### 3. Quality

This axis describes the commitment to delivering high-quality products and experiences that solve user problems and meet business goals.

#### Product Manager

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :---- | :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Follows           | Writes clear and complete user stories that can be easily understood by the team. They ensure that acceptance criteria are well-defined and that the team has the information it needs to build the right thing.                                                                                                                                       |
| 2     | Consistent        | Ensures that the features the team builds effectively solve the user's problem and meet the business goals. They are diligent about validating their assumptions with data and user feedback.                                                                                                                                                          |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Is accountable for the overall success and user experience of their product area. They use data and user feedback to continuously improve the product and ensure it meets a high bar for quality. They are a key partner to design and engineering in ensuring a high-quality outcome.                                                                |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Introduces processes and practices that improve product quality across the organization, such as creating a new framework for user feedback, a new process for A/B testing, or a new way of measuring product quality. They are a force multiplier for product quality.                                                                              |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Establishes the company-wide bar for product excellence. They are a key voice in defining what it means to ship a high-quality product at the company. Their work and influence ensure that the company's reputation for quality is a competitive advantage.                                                                                            |

#### Product Designer

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :---- | :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Follows           | Delivers polished designs that are free of errors and follow the established design system and brand guidelines. They are diligent about the details and ensure that their work is of high quality.                                                                                                                                                     |
| 2     | Consistent        | Creates designs that are intuitive, easy to use, and aesthetically pleasing. They are a consistent advocate for the user's needs and ensure that the team is building a product that is both functional and delightful.                                                                                                                            |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Creates elegant and cohesive user experiences that are a model for others. They are a champion for high design standards and help to raise the bar for design quality across the team. They are a key partner to the PM and engineering in ensuring a high-quality user experience.                                                                   |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Creates and evangelizes design systems and practices that improve design quality and consistency across the entire organization. They are a key driver of design excellence and help to ensure that the company is delivering a world-class user experience.                                                                                           |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Is a key voice in defining what it means to ship a high-quality product at the company. Their work and influence ensure that the company's reputation for design is a competitive advantage.                                                                                                                                                           |

### 4. Execution

This axis describes the ability to deliver results and ship products in a timely and predictable manner.

#### Product Manager

| Level | Title         | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner       | Effectively manages a backlog for small, well-defined tasks. They can clearly communicate the status of their work and are learning to break down problems and estimate work.                                                                                                                                                                      |
| 2     | Delivers      | Works effectively with an engineering team to ship small-to-medium features in a predictable and timely manner. They are able to manage their own work and to deliver on their commitments.                                                                                                                                                         |
| 3     | Owner         | Reliably ships large and complex features and products from discovery to launch. They are skilled at managing dependencies, mitigating risks, and communicating with stakeholders. They are a reliable and predictable leader who can be counted on to deliver on their commitments.                                                                   |
| 4     | Orchestrates  | Orchestrates the launch of complex, multi-team products. They are a master of project management and are able to keep many different streams of work on track, even in the face of ambiguity. They are a leader who can deliver product at scale.                                                                                                       |
| 5     | Scales        | Creates and improves the processes and tools that the entire organization uses to ship high-quality products predictably and efficiently. They are a strategic leader who helps the company make smart investments in its product development process.                                                                                                   |

#### Product Designer

| Level | Title         | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner       | Delivers design assets on time and to spec. They are reliable and responsive in supporting the team's execution and are learning to manage their workload.                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| 2     | Delivers      | Manages their own design work for features, ensuring that designs are delivered on time and that the team has what it needs to build them. They are a reliable and predictable contributor to the team's execution.                                                                                                                                     |
| 3     | Owner         | Owns the design execution for large and complex projects. They are able to effectively manage their own time and the design process to ensure high-quality designs are delivered on time, even when faced with ambiguity and tight deadlines.                                                                                                        |
| 4     | Orchestrates  | Manages the design execution for projects that span multiple designers or teams. They are able to effectively coordinate the work of others and ensure a cohesive and high-quality user experience, even on complex, multi-team projects.                                                                                                             |
| 5     | Scales        | Creates and improves the processes and tools that the entire organization uses to design and ship high-quality products. They are a strategic leader who helps the company make smart investments in its design process.                                                                                                                                |

### 5. People

This axis describes the ability to collaborate, communicate, and grow oneself and others, contributing to a healthy and high-performing team culture.

#### Product Manager

| Level | Title           | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| :---- | :-------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner         | Communicates clearly with engineering and design peers, remaining open to feedback. They build trust by being reliable, listening actively, and learning how to articulate product requirements effectively.                                                                                                                                   |
| 2     | Contributor     | Actively participates in team rituals. Provides respectful, constructive feedback to their cross-functional peers. Is a good collaborator and partner to engineering and design, helping the team work together effectively.                                                                                                                        |
| 3     | Mentor          | Formally coaches and mentors Associate PMs. They help resolve conflicts within their product team constructively, improve collaboration practices, and take an active role in onboarding new team members.                                                                                                                                  |
| 4     | Leader          | Leads cross-functional project teams by setting a clear vision and managing contributors to achieve shared goals. They build consensus across groups with differing priorities, mediate disagreements, and actively support the growth of other mentors.                                                                                         |
| 5     | Culture Builder | Shapes a healthy, high-performing product culture across the organization. They champion collaboration, customer-centricity, and inclusion. They act as a role model for the company's values and play a key role in attracting, retaining, and developing exceptional product talent.                                                          |

#### Product Designer

| Level | Title           | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| :---- | :-------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner         | Communicates design ideas clearly to their immediate team, remaining open to feedback. They build trust by being a reliable partner in the design process and are learning to articulate the rationale behind their design decisions.                                                                                                               |
| 2     | Contributor     | Actively participates in design critiques and team rituals. They provide respectful, constructive feedback that helps their peers improve their work. They are a good collaborator and partner to PM and engineering, ensuring design is an integral part of the product development process.                                                        |
| 3     | Mentor          | Formally coaches and mentors junior designers. They lead design critiques, help resolve disagreements constructively, improve design practices, and take an active role in onboarding new designers.                                                                                                                                           |
| 4     | Leader          | Leads the design effort on large, cross-functional projects. They align multiple designers and stakeholders on a cohesive design vision and manage contributors to achieve a high-quality user experience. They are a key voice in ensuring a user-centered approach across multiple teams.                                                    |
| 5     | Culture Builder | Shapes a healthy, high-performing design culture across the organization. They champion design thinking, user-centricity, and a culture of critique and collaboration. They act as a role model for the company's values and play a key role in attracting, retaining, and developing exceptional design talent.                                      |

### 6. Influence

This axis describes the scope and impact of one's contributions, from an individual task to shaping the entire industry.

#### Product Manager

| Level | Title      | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| :---- | :--------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Local      | Work primarily impacts the immediate features they are assigned to. Their sphere of influence is their own backlog and user stories, and success is defined by delivering their assigned tasks effectively.                                                                                                                                     |
| 2     | Team       | Contributions improve the team's ability to achieve its goals. They own the roadmap and success of a small product area, and their work directly enables the team to be more effective.                                                                                                                                                            |
| 3     | Multi-team | Work shapes the direction of a significant product area. They provide guidance outside their immediate scope and own product strategies that create leverage for other teams, establishing best practices that are adopted more broadly.                                                                                                          |
| 4     | Organization | Defines strategies, standards, or platforms that have a significant, positive impact on the entire product organization. Their decisions and leadership on cross-cutting initiatives shape the direction of a large group of PMs and deliver substantial business value.                                                                          |
| 5     | Industry   | Is recognized as an authority beyond the company for their contributions. They shape external communities and advance the state of the art in product management through influential work, publications, or presentations, enhancing both their own and the company's reputation.                                                                    |

#### Product Designer

| Level | Title      | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| :---- | :--------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Local      | Work primarily impacts the specific UI components and screens they design. Their sphere of influence is their own design files, and success is defined by delivering high-quality design assets that meet the requirements.                                                                                                                      |
| 2     | Team       | Contributions improve the user experience of the team's product. They own the design for specific features and their work directly enables the team to deliver a more usable and delightful product.                                                                                                                                             |
| 3     | Multi-team | Work shapes the user experience of a significant product area. They own and maintain design systems that are used by multiple teams, creating a more cohesive and consistent user experience across the product.                                                                                                                                    |
| 4     | Organization | Defines design strategies, standards, and systems that have a significant, positive impact on the entire organization. Their decisions and leadership on cross-cutting initiatives shape the user experience of the entire product and deliver substantial business value.                                                                          |
| 5     | Industry   | Is recognized as an authority beyond the company for their contributions. They shape external communities and advance the state of the art in product design through influential work, open-source design systems, or presentations, enhancing both their own and the company's reputation.                                                              |
