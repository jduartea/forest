# Data - Axes

This document defines the axes of progression for all roles within the Data discipline, including **Data Analysts**, **Data Engineers**, and **Data Scientists**. It provides role-specific descriptions for each axis and level across both IC and Manager tracks.

### 1. Modeling

This axis describes the ability to translate raw data and business problems into structured, understandable, and actionable forms, whether through analytics, systems, or algorithms.

#### Data Analyst

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| :---- | :---------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Builds simple dashboards and queries from well-defined requirements. Their work is focused on answering specific, scoped questions by learning the company's core data sources and tools. Success means delivering accurate information that helps their immediate team.                                                                       |
| 2     | Contributor | Creates insightful analyses that answer ambiguous business questions. They can skillfully combine multiple data sources, identify the right metrics, and tell a compelling story with data that helps their team understand performance and opportunities.                                                                                        |
| 3     | Owner       | Owns the analytics for a specific product or business area. They proactively identify the most important questions to answer, design and conduct complex A/B tests, and deliver deep-dive analyses that move beyond "what" happened to explaining "why." Their insights directly influence their team's roadmap and strategy.                     |
| 4     | Architect   | Designs and implements a cohesive analytics strategy for a large part of the business. They define how success is measured for complex, multi-team initiatives and create frameworks and mental models that help the organization make better decisions. Their work influences the direction of multiple teams.                                    |
| 5     | Strategist  | Defines the company's overall strategy for how it uses data to find and act on business opportunities. They are a thought leader who identifies systemic opportunities and threats, and their work shapes the strategic direction of the entire company.                                                                                         |

#### Data Engineer

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| :---- | :---------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Implements straightforward data pipeline tasks within an existing architecture. They focus on learning the team's data engineering patterns, tools, and data sources, ensuring their code is correct and follows established best practices.                                                                                                    |
| 2     | Contributor | Builds and maintains reliable data pipelines for a single domain. They can debug and optimize moderately complex data flows, understand data lineage, and ensure that the data they provide is accurate, timely, and trustworthy for its consumers.                                                                                                |
| 3     | Owner       | Owns a significant component of the data platform (e.g., the data warehouse, a streaming data system). They design scalable, maintainable, and cost-effective data systems, making sound architectural trade-offs. They are accountable for the reliability, performance, and cost of the data products they own.                                 |
| 4     | Architect   | Designs and builds major new components of the data platform that provide significant new capabilities to the organization. Their work serves multiple teams and abstracts away complex problems, making it easier for everyone to work with data effectively. They are a go-to expert for data architecture.                                |
| 5     | Strategist  | Defines the long-term vision and architecture for the company's entire data platform. They ensure the platform can scale to meet the company's future needs and that the right investments are being made in data infrastructure, tooling, and governance.                                                                                        |

#### Data Scientist

| Level | Title       | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| :---- | :---------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner     | Implements feature engineering and model training tasks with guidance. They focus on learning the team's machine learning frameworks, data sources, and model lifecycle, and can execute well-defined parts of a larger modeling project.                                                                                                       |
| 2     | Contributor | Can independently build and validate a machine learning model for a well-scoped problem. They understand the trade-offs between different modeling approaches and can select the right tool for the job. They are responsible for the quality and reproducibility of their models.                                                                |
| 3     | Owner       | Owns the end-to-end development and deployment of a machine learning model that solves a core business problem. They make sound decisions about model architecture, feature selection, and evaluation metrics, balancing complexity and performance. They are responsible for the model's impact and performance in production.                     |
| 4     | Architect   | Designs and builds complex machine learning systems that are a core part of the company's product or strategy. Their work often involves solving novel and ambiguous problems, and they are a go-to expert for ML system design. They often create frameworks or platforms that enable other teams to build models more effectively.           |
| 5     | Strategist  | Defines the company's strategy for machine learning and artificial intelligence. They identify new opportunities to use ML to create significant business value and are a key voice in the company's long-term technology vision. Their work often creates new and durable competitive advantages for the company.                                |

### 2. Mastery

This axis describes the depth of technical expertise in the tools, techniques, and theoretical foundations of a specific data discipline.

#### Data Analyst

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :---- | :----------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the fundamentals of SQL and a data visualization tool (e.g., Tableau, Looker). Their focus is on writing correct queries and building clear, simple visualizations that accurately represent the data.                                                                                                                                        |
| 2     | Practitioner | Is proficient in SQL and can write complex, efficient queries against large datasets. They are skilled at using data visualization tools to create compelling dashboards and reports that are both insightful and easy to understand. They have a solid grasp of descriptive and inferential statistics.                                            |
| 3     | Specialist   | Is an expert in a specific domain of analytics (e.g., product analytics, marketing analytics, experimentation). Has deep statistical knowledge and can apply advanced techniques to answer complex questions. They are a go-to person for their deep domain knowledge and analytical expertise.                                                          |
| 4     | Expert       | Improves analytics practices across the organization by mentoring others, introducing new tools or methodologies, or establishing best practices. They are often a deep expert in a cross-cutting area like experimentation, causal inference, or data storytelling.                                                                                 |
| 5     | Innovator    | Is an industry leader in data analytics. They may develop new analytical techniques or frameworks that advance the state of the art. Their work helps to build the company's reputation as a leader in data-driven decision making.                                                                                                                |

#### Data Engineer

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :---- | :----------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the fundamentals of a programming language (e.g., Python, Scala), a data processing framework (e.g., Spark), and a workflow orchestrator (e.g., Airflow). Their focus is on writing correct, maintainable code that adheres to team standards.                                                                                                  |
| 2     | Practitioner | Is proficient in building and debugging data pipelines. They can write clean, efficient, and maintainable code and have a strong grasp of the tools and frameworks used by the team. They can independently diagnose and resolve most common data pipeline issues.                                                                                   |
| 3     | Specialist   | Is an expert in a specific domain of data engineering, such as streaming data, data warehousing, or data governance. They apply this deep knowledge to solve complex technical challenges and are a go-to person for their expertise in that area.                                                                                                   |
| 4     | Expert       | Has deep expertise across multiple data engineering domains. They design and build reusable tools, libraries, and frameworks that improve the productivity and effectiveness of the entire data engineering organization. They are a force multiplier for their peers.                                                                                |
| 5     | Innovator    | Is an industry leader in data engineering. They may pioneer new approaches to building scalable and reliable data systems, or contribute significantly to open source projects. Their work helps to establish the company as a leader in data engineering.                                                                                           |

#### Data Scientist

| Level | Title        | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| :---- | :----------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner      | Learns the fundamentals of machine learning theory, a programming language (e.g., Python), and common ML libraries (e.g., scikit-learn, TensorFlow). Their focus is on understanding the core concepts and applying them correctly to solve well-defined problems.                                                                                   |
| 2     | Practitioner | Is proficient in building and evaluating machine learning models. They can write clean and efficient code for data processing, feature engineering, and modeling. They have a strong grasp of the theoretical foundations of the models they use and can explain their workings to others.                                                              |
| 3     | Specialist   | Is an expert in a specific domain of machine learning (e.g., NLP, computer vision, recommender systems). They are up-to-date on the latest research in their field and can apply it to solve real-world problems. They are a go-to person for their deep expertise in their domain.                                                                    |
| 4     | Expert       | Has deep expertise across multiple machine learning domains. They design and build reusable tools, libraries, and platforms that improve the productivity and effectiveness of the entire data science organization. They often mentor other data scientists on advanced modeling techniques.                                                             |
| 5     | Innovator    | Is an industry leader in machine learning. They may develop new modeling techniques that advance the state of the art, or publish their work in top-tier conferences. Their work helps to establish the company as a leader in machine learning and AI.                                                                                              |

### 3. Quality

This axis describes the commitment to rigor, accuracy, and reliability in all aspects of data work, from analysis to production systems.

#### Data Analyst

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :---- | :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Follows           | Produces accurate and clear analyses, relying on feedback and review to improve the quality of their work. They are diligent about checking their queries and results, but may not always anticipate subtle data quality issues.                                                                                                                          |
| 2     | Consistent        | Consistently delivers high-quality analyses that are well-documented, easy to understand, and reproducible. They are proactive about identifying and highlighting potential data quality issues and are a reliable source of truth for their domain.                                                                                                     |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Champions a culture of data quality and analytical rigor on their team. They go beyond just delivering analyses to ensuring that the underlying data is accurate and that the team is using it correctly to make decisions. They are a key partner in improving the team's data literacy.                                                                 |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Designs and implements frameworks, tools, and processes that improve the quality and reliability of analytics across the organization. This could include building a new A/B testing framework, creating a new data dictionary, or developing a new process for data quality monitoring.                                                               |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Establishes the company-wide bar for analytical rigor and data quality. They are a key voice in the company's data governance strategy and help to ensure that the company is using data in a responsible and ethical way.                                                                                                                              |

#### Data Engineer

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :---- | :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Follows           | Writes code that is functional and correct, relying on code reviews and testing to identify issues. Their focus is on delivering working code that meets the immediate requirements.                                                                                                                                                                      |
| 2     | Consistent        | Writes code that is maintainable, well-tested, and documented. Their work is reliable and rarely causes production issues. They are proactive about monitoring their pipelines and can quickly diagnose and fix problems when they arise.                                                                                                                    |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Champions a culture of data quality and engineering excellence on their team. They are a key driver of best practices for testing, monitoring, and alerting. They are proactive about identifying and paying down technical debt.                                                                                                                             |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Designs and implements systems and frameworks that improve the quality and reliability of the entire data platform. This could include building a new data testing framework, a new data lineage system, or a new system for anomaly detection. They make it easier for all engineers to build high-quality data products.                                |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Establishes the company-wide bar for data engineering excellence. They are a key voice in defining the company's standards for data quality, reliability, and security. They help to create a culture where every engineer feels ownership over the quality of the data platform.                                                                      |

#### Data Scientist

| Level | Title             | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| :---- | :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Follows           | Produces models that are correct and well-documented, relying on feedback and review to improve their modeling process. They are diligent about following the team's best practices for model development and validation.                                                                                                                               |
| 2     | Consistent        | Consistently delivers high-quality models that are well-tested, reproducible, and easy to understand. They are proactive about identifying potential issues with their models (e.g., bias, fairness) and are transparent about their limitations.                                                                                                         |
| 3     | Raises the Bar    | Champions a culture of modeling excellence and scientific rigor on their team. They are a key driver of best practices for model validation, A/B testing, and monitoring. They are proactive about ensuring that their team's models are not only accurate but also fair, interpretable, and aligned with the company's ethical principles.             |
| 4     | Multiplies Quality | Designs and implements frameworks and platforms that improve the quality and reliability of machine learning across the organization. This could include building a new feature store, a new model monitoring system, or a new framework for responsible AI. They make it easier for all data scientists to build high-quality, responsible models. |
| 5     | Defines Excellence | Establishes the company-wide bar for machine learning excellence. They are a key voice in defining the company's strategy for responsible AI and help to ensure that the company is using machine learning in a way that is both innovative and ethical.                                                                                               |

### 4. Execution

This axis describes the ability to deliver results reliably and predictably, from completing individual tasks to driving large, complex projects.

#### Data Analyst

| Level | Title         | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner       | Completes assigned analyses and reports status clearly and on time. They are learning to manage their workload and to communicate their progress effectively to the team.                                                                                                                                                                              |
| 2     | Delivers      | Can independently manage a small analytics project from start to finish, including defining the scope, gathering the data, conducting the analysis, and presenting the results. They are a reliable and predictable contributor to the team's goals.                                                                                                   |
| 3     | Owner         | Owns the analytics for a large feature or product area. They are responsible for defining the analytics roadmap for their area and for ensuring that the team is learning what it needs to in order to make smart decisions. They are a key partner to the product manager and tech lead.                                                              |
| 4     | Orchestrates  | Manages the analytics for a major product launch or a complex, cross-functional initiative. They are able to coordinate the work of multiple analysts and stakeholders to deliver a cohesive and impactful set of insights. They are a leader who can deliver analytical insights at scale.                                                               |
| 5     | Scales        | Creates and improves the processes and tools that the entire organization uses to conduct analytics effectively and efficiently. They are a strategic leader who helps the company make smart investments in analytics.                                                                                                                                  |

#### Data Engineer

| Level | Title         | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---- | :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Learner       | Completes assigned coding tasks and reports status clearly and on time. They are learning to break down problems into smaller pieces and to estimate their work.                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| 2     | Delivers      | Can independently build and ship a new data pipeline of moderate complexity. They are able to manage their own work and to deliver on their commitments in a timely and predictable manner.                                                                                                                                                             |
| 3     | Owner         | Owns a significant part of the data platform. They are responsible for the planning, execution, and delivery of the roadmap for their area. They are a reliable and predictable leader who can be counted on to deliver on their commitments.                                                                                                               |
| 4     | Orchestrates  | Manages a large, cross-team data engineering project. They are able to coordinate the work of multiple engineers and teams to deliver a complex and impactful project. They are a leader who can deliver data engineering projects at scale.                                                                                                              |
| 5     | Scales        | Creates and improves the processes and tools that the entire organization uses to build and operate data systems. They are a strategic leader who helps the company make smart investments in data engineering.                                                                                                                                          |

| :---- | :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |

They are learning to break down complex modeling problems into smaller pieces and to estimate their work.                                                                                                                                                                  |
| 2     | Delivers      | Can independently build and ship a new machine learning model of moderate complexity. They are able to manage their own work and to deliver on their commitments in a timely and predictable manner.                                                                                                                                                    |
| 3     | Owner         | Owns a machine learning model that is a key part of the product. They are responsible for the planning, execution, and delivery of the roadmap for their model. ### 5. People

This axis describes the ability to collaborate, communicate, and grow oneself and others, contributing to a healthy and high-performing team culture.

#### Data Analyst, Data Engineer, & Data Scientist

| Level | Title           | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| :---- | :-------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Learner         | Communicates clearly with teammates and remains open to feedback, seeking help when stuck. They build trust by being reliable, listening actively in team discussions, and responding to feedback with a constructive, growth-oriented mindset.                                                                                                 |
| 2     | Contributor     | Actively participates in team rituals. Provides respectful, constructive feedback that helps teammates improve. Is a good collaborator and informally shares knowledge, making the team more effective.                                                                                                                                     |
| 3     | Mentor          | Formally coaches teammates and other peers through thoughtful feedback, and knowledge-sharing sessions. Helps resolve team conflicts constructively, improves collaboration practices, and takes an active role in onboarding and supporting the growth of junior team members.                                                                 |
| 4     | Leader          | Leads project teams by setting clear direction and managing contributors to achieve shared goals. They build consensus across groups, mediate disagreements, and actively support the growth of other mentors, fostering an inclusive environment and acting as a trusted voice in cross-functional forums.                                        |
| 5     | Culture Builder | Shapes a healthy, high-performing data culture across the organization. Champions collaboration, inclusion, and psychological safety. Acts as a role model for the company's values and plays a key role in attracting, retaining, and developing exceptional talent.                                                               |

### 6. Influence

This axis describes the scope and impact of one's contributions, from an individual task to shaping the entire industry.

#### Data Analyst, Data Engineer, & Data Scientist

| Level | Title      | Behaviors & Outcomes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| :---- | :--------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Local      | Work primarily impacts their immediate tasks and the features they are assigned to. Their sphere of influence is their own work, and success is defined by learning how their individual contributions fit into the team's larger goals.                                                                                                        |
| 2     | Team       | Contributions improve the team's ability to achieve its goals. They own analyses, pipelines, or models that their teammates depend on, and their work directly enables the team to be more effective.                                                                                                                                       |
| 3     | Multi-team | Work shapes the direction of a product area or a business unit. They provide guidance outside their immediate scope and own data products that create leverage for other teams, establishing best practices that are adopted more broadly.                                                                                                      |
| 4     | Organization | Defines strategies, standards, or platforms that have a significant, positive impact on the entire organization. Their decisions and leadership on cross-cutting initiatives shape the direction of a large group of data professionals and deliver substantial business value.                                                              |
| 5     | Industry   | Is recognized as an authority beyond the company for their contributions. They shape external communities and advance the state of the art through influential work, publications, or presentations, enhancing both their own and the company's reputation.                                                                                          |
