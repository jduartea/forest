# HR - Axes

This document defines the axes of progression for the **HRBP** role.

### 1. Acquisition

This axis describes the ability to attract, select, and hire the best talent, as well as building a strong employer brand.

#### HRBP

| Level | Title | Behaviors & Outcomes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Support | Supports hiring processes by scheduling and coordinating interviews, screening resumes for junior or intern roles under guidance, and ensuring a positive, seamless candidate experience. Assists with employer branding events (e.g., job fairs, meetups) and helps create social media content. Diligently tracks hiring metrics in the ATS (Applicant Tracking System) and maintains data integrity to support reporting. |
| 2 | Partner | Manages full-cycle recruiting for specific individual contributor and manager roles within their business unit. Partners deeply with hiring managers to define role requirements, craft inclusive job descriptions, and design structured, bias-free interview plans and evaluation rubrics. Actively contributes to the employer branding strategy by representing the company at industry events, optimizing candidate communications, and training interviewers on best practices. |
| 3 | Lead | Owns the end-to-end talent acquisition strategy for major business units or the entire organization. Partners with senior leadership on long-term workforce planning, headcount forecasting, and budget allocation. Drivers major, high-impact employer branding initiatives to position the company as a top employer in the market. Mentors hiring managers and other HR team members on advanced interviewing techniques, bar-raising strategies, and reducing unconscious bias in the selection process. |

### 2. Engagement

This axis describes the ability to foster a positive work environment, drive employee engagement, and shape the company culture.

#### HRBP

| Level | Title | Behaviors & Outcomes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Support | Organizes and executes team events, social activities, and wellbeing initiatives that foster connection. Supports the administration of engagement surveys (e.g., setting up the tool, reminding participants) and helps compile basic results. Acts as a consistent "culture carrier," fostering a sense of belonging, inclusivity, and psychological safety in daily interactions. Helps track engagement scores and participation rates to identify early trends. |
| 2 | Partner | Deeply analyzes engagement survey data to identify key drivers, trends, and areas for improvement within their specific groups. Partners with managers to create and drive specific, measurable engagement action plans that address feedback. Champions culture and wellbeing programs, ensuring they are accessible, relevant, and impactful for their teams. Resolves routine employee relations issues efficiently and helps teams interpret and act on engagement data to improve overall team health. |
| 3 | Lead | Designs the overarching culture and engagement strategy for the organization, linking it to business goals. Leads comprehensive wellbeing initiatives that support physical, mental, and emotional health at scale. Advises executive leadership on organizational health, cultural alignment, and retention strategies. Drives company-wide culture programs that reinforce core values and boost engagement. Handles complex, high-risk employee relations cases and sets the standard for a positive, inclusive, and high-performing work environment. |

### 3. Development

This axis describes the ability to support the growth and development of employees and managers.

#### HRBP

| Level | Title | Behaviors & Outcomes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Support | Administers training programs and manages the Learning Management System (LMS) or training records. Supports the logistics of performance review cycles (e.g., communications, system setup), ensuring timelines are met. Helps employees track their growth plans and answers questions about available development resources, budget usage, and internal mobility processes. |
| 2 | Partner | Coaches managers on how to have effective development conversations, give constructive feedback, and build high-performing teams. Facilitates workshops and training sessions on core skills (e.g., feedback, goal setting). Drives the performance management process for their designated area, ensuring calibration, quality, and consistent use of evaluation rubrics. Identifies training needs within their teams and proposes targeted, evidence-based solutions to close skill gaps. |
| 3 | Lead | Designs comprehensive talent development frameworks, including competency models, career ladders, and leadership development programs. Leads succession planning for critical roles and leadership positions to ensure business continuity. Coaches senior leaders on organizational design, talent strategy, and executive presence. Builds scalable programs that democratize access to learning and development across the entire organization, measuring impact on business performance. |

### 4. Operations

This axis describes the ability to manage HR processes, systems, compensation, benefits, and office operations efficiently.

#### HRBP

| Level | Title | Behaviors & Outcomes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Support | Serves as the first point of contact for employee queries regarding benefits, payroll, and policy. Updates HR systems (HRIS) with accurate employee data (new hires, changes, exits) in a timely manner. Manages daily office logistics, supplies, and facilities to ensure a functional workspace. Follows defined HR processes diligently to ensure compliance with company policies and data accuracy. |
| 2 | Partner | Optimizes HR processes to improve efficiency, reduce friction, and enhance the user experience. Manages compensation review cycles for their business area, ensuring fairness, internal equity, and alignment with the budget. Oversees office management, facilities, and vendor relationships to ensure a safe, productive, and inspiring environment. Resolves complex operational issues and ensures compliance with local labor laws and regulations. |
| 3 | Lead | Designs the total rewards strategy, including compensation philosophy, salary bands, and competitive benefits packages. Leads the implementation and integration of major new HR systems (HRIS, ATS, Performance) and tools. Defines operational policies, compliance standards, and remote/hybrid work guidelines. Ensures people operations are scalable, robust, and automated where possible to support the company's growth trajectory and minimize risk. |

### 5. Organization

This axis describes the ability to design effective organizational structures, improve ways of working, and manage change.

#### HRBP

| Level | Title | Behaviors & Outcomes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Support | Supports the implementation of new ways of working, process changes, and organizational announcements. Helps document team norms, standard operating procedures (SOPs), and role responsibilities. Assists with the communication and logistics of change management initiatives, ensuring messages are delivered clearly and consistently. |
| 2 | Partner | Facilitates organizational design discussions with managers to improve team structure, roles, and reporting lines. Diagnoses team workflow issues, bottlenecks, and communication breakdowns, helping to improve norms and collaboration practices. Advises managers on change management strategies to ensure smooth transitions during times of growth, reorganization, or strategic pivots. |
| 3 | Lead | Leads complex organizational design and restructuring efforts to align with the evolving business strategy. Defines future ways of working, organizational models, and governance structures. Drives large-scale change management programs, ensuring stakeholder alignment, minimizing disruption, and maintaining morale. Strategic partner to the C-suite on evolving the organization's structure to meet long-term goals and market challenges. |
